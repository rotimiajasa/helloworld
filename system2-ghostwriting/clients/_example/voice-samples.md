# Voice Samples — _example

Paste anything they've already written publicly. The more raw, the better — Slack messages, voice transcripts, tweet drafts. We want patterns, not polish.

## Samples

### Sample 1 — LinkedIn post (May 2026)

> Last Tuesday, the head nurse at one of our partner hospitals called me in tears.
>
> Their EMR was down for 3 hours. 47 admissions stalled.
>
> Not because of our software — their internet provider had cut over to a new gateway and didn't tell anyone.
>
> By 10pm we had a hotfix that lets the app run fully offline and sync when the connection comes back. Shipped to all 12 hospitals by Thursday.
>
> Two weeks later, that nurse texts me: "We've stopped checking if the system is up. We just use it."
>
> That sentence is worth more than any feature roadmap.

### Sample 2 — internal Slack message
> Team — we're not pivoting away from hospitals to clinics. I know that's been floated. The reason is boring: clinics don't pay. Hospitals do. Until that changes, the pipe stays. If we want to test clinics, we test it as an opt-in pilot, not a pivot.

### Sample 3 — voice note transcript
> [00:00] Yeah so the thing about Lagos hospital tech is everyone thinks it's a software problem. It's not. It's a … it's a power problem. It's a "the doctor's been on for 18 hours and her hands are shaking" problem. Anyone building for this market who hasn't sat in a hospital reception at 2am for a week — they're going to ship the wrong thing.

## Voice tells we noticed

- Specific timestamps, names, numbers ("47 admissions", "12 hospitals", "Tuesday").
- Plain language. No jargon.
- Stories beat opinions.
- Self-aware ("I know that's been floated" — not "let me clarify our strategy").
