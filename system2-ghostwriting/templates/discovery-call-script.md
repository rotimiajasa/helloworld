# Discovery Call Script (30 minutes)

The orchestrator generates a custom version per prospect using their recent posts. This is the canonical structure.

## Pre-call (Claude generates this 1 hr before)

A 1-page brief covering:
- Their last 10 LinkedIn posts (titles + key claims).
- Engagement pattern (avg impressions, top post, low post).
- Apparent business focus / what they're selling.
- Open questions you should ask.

## On the call

### Minute 0–3: Frame
> "I'll keep this to 30 minutes max. I'd like to understand your business and what you want LinkedIn to do for you, then I'll tell you honestly whether what I do is a fit. If it's not, I'll point you somewhere better. Sound OK?"

### Minute 3–18: Discovery (15 min, you mostly listen)

Ask in this order:
1. **What does your business actually do, and who's the perfect client?**
2. **What's LinkedIn currently doing for the business?** (Listen for: nothing, some inbound, hires, raises.)
3. **What would have to be true in 6 months for you to say "LinkedIn was worth it"?**
   → This is the goal. Write it down verbatim.
4. **What's stopped you from posting consistently so far?**
   → 90% say "time" or "I don't know what to write."
5. **Who do you LOVE following on LinkedIn? Why?**
   → This tells you their taste — critical for ghostwriting.
6. **What topics would you NEVER post about?**
   → Religion, politics, competitors, anything that'd hurt fundraise/sale.

### Minute 18–25: Mirror + Pitch

> "Here's what I heard: {{paraphrase 3 of their goals back}}. The thing I'd do differently from most agencies is [specific to them]. I'm priced at [Pro tier first — anchor high]. Here's exactly what's included.
>
> If we worked together, week 1 would be a 60-min recorded interview. That gives me your voice. From week 2, you'd see 4 drafts a week — you approve in batch, I schedule. By month 2, you'd have 30+ posts live and a baseline to compare future months against."

### Minute 25–30: Close

Choose one of:
- **Direct ask**: "Want to start? I'd send the contract and we kick off Monday."
- **Soft ask**: "Want to think on it? I'll send a 1-page proposal today, then we can decide on Wednesday."
- **Walk-away**: "I'm not sure we're a fit because [reason]. Here's someone better: [referral]."

The walk-away closes more deals than the soft ask. It signals integrity.

## Post-call (Claude does this within 2 hours)

- Generates personalized proposal from the call notes.
- Drafts 1 sample post in their voice based on what they said on the call.
- Drafts the follow-up email with proposal + sample attached.

You review, send. Total post-call work: 5 minutes.
