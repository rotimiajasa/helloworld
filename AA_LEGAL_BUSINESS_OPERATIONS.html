Section 03 — The Engagement Lifecycle

End-to-End Client Workflow

Every file at AA Legal flows through a disciplined 12-stage process designed to meet Law Society of Alberta standards, ensure consistent quality, and protect both the client and the firm.

Initial Enquiry & Lead Capture

Prospective clients reach us by phone, email (yemi.a@aalegal.ca), website form (aalegal.ca), referral, or walk-in. Every enquiry is logged in the CRM with source, matter type, and urgency.

  • Multi-channel intake: phone, email, web, referral
  • Same-day acknowledgement target
  • Triage to the appropriate practice area

Conflict Check & KYC

Before any substantive discussion, we run a conflict-of-interest search against current and historical clients, and complete client identification under the Law Society’s Client Identification & Verification Rules.

  • Name, address, occupation, ID verification
  • Source-of-funds enquiry where applicable
  • Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening

Consultation & Scope Definition

An in-person, telephone, or video consultation explores the client’s objectives, legal issues, risks, and possible strategies. We provide a candid assessment of merits, costs, and timelines.

  • 30–60 minute consult (free or flat-fee)
  • Issue spotting & preliminary strategy
  • Discussion of fees, billing & expectations

Retainer & Engagement Letter

A written retainer agreement defines scope, fee structure (hourly, flat, contingency, block), trust deposit, billing cycle, and the rights and duties of both parties.

  • E-signed retainer (DocuSign / Adobe Sign)
  • Trust deposit collected via Interac, cheque, or wire
  • Trust ledger opened per LSA trust accounting rules

File Opening & Matter Setup

A new matter is created in the practice management system with a unique file number, deadlines calendared, document folder created, and the responsible lawyer / paralegal assigned.

  • Matter plan & task list
  • Limitation & court deadlines diarised
  • Document templates pre-loaded

Discovery, Research & Document Collection

We gather evidence, financial disclosure, contracts, identification, corporate records, or whatever the file requires; conduct legal research; and prepare a working file plan.

  • Secure client portal for document upload
  • Statutory & case-law research (CanLII, Westlaw, Lexis)
  • Witness interviews where applicable

Strategy & Drafting

The lawyer drafts pleadings, contracts, wills, agreements, letters of demand, or immigration applications. All drafts undergo internal QA before client review.

  • Precedent library & clause bank
  • Two-stage internal review
  • Plain-language client summaries

Negotiation & Representation

We represent the client before opposing counsel, government agencies (IRCC, Land Titles), tribunals, or courts — pursuing settlement first where appropriate.

  • Mediation & collaborative practice
  • Court appearances & hearings
  • Regular client status updates

Execution & Closing

Documents are signed, registered, filed, or submitted. Funds are disbursed from trust. Title transfers, divorce judgments, probate grants, or PR confirmations are obtained.

  • E-signing & remote commissioning
  • Trust disbursement with full reconciliation
  • Registration with Land Titles, courts, IRCC

Billing & Collections

Time entries are captured daily; invoices are issued per the retainer schedule; trust funds are applied per LSA rules; outstanding receivables are followed up systematically.

  • Detailed dockets & transparent narratives
  • Electronic invoicing & online payment
  • Monthly trust reconciliation

File Closing & Reporting Letter

Upon completion we issue a reporting letter summarising the work done, outcomes, and any ongoing client obligations (e.g., updating a will after major life events).

  • Final reporting letter
  • Return of original documents
  • Closed-file archiving (10+ years per LSA rules)

Post-Engagement & Client Retention

We request feedback, invite Google reviews, schedule periodic check-ins (estate refresh, immigration renewal), and continue value-add via newsletters and seminars.

  • NPS survey & testimonials
  • Annual “legal check-up” reminders
  • Referrals & alumni program
