Section 03 · The Engagement Lifecycle

Twelve Stages of a Client Matter

❦

Every file at AA Legal flows through a disciplined twelve-stage process. The framework is designed to meet Law Society of Alberta standards, ensure consistent quality, and protect both the client and the firm.

Initial Enquiry & Lead Capture

Prospective clients reach us by phone, email, website form, referral, or walk-in. Every enquiry is logged in the CRM with source, matter type, and urgency.

  • Multi-channel intake: phone, email, web, referral
  • Same-day acknowledgement target
  • Triage to the appropriate practice area

Conflict Check & KYC

Before any substantive discussion, a conflict-of-interest search is run, and client identification is completed under the Law Society's Client Identification and Verification Rules.

  • Name, address, occupation, and ID verification
  • Source-of-funds enquiry where applicable
  • Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening

Consultation & Scope Definition

An in-person, telephone, or video consultation explores the client's objectives, risks, and possible strategies. The client receives a candid assessment of merits, costs, and timelines.

  • 30–60 minute consultation (free or flat-fee)
  • Issue spotting and preliminary strategy
  • Discussion of fees, billing, and expectations

Retainer & Engagement Letter

A written retainer agreement defines scope, fee structure (hourly, flat, contingency, block), trust deposit, billing cycle, and the rights and duties of both parties.

  • E-signed retainer via DocuSign or Adobe Sign
  • Trust deposit collected via Interac, cheque, or wire
  • Trust ledger opened per LSA trust accounting rules

File Opening & Matter Setup

A new matter is created in the practice management system with a unique file number, deadlines calendared, document folder created, and the responsible lawyer and paralegal assigned.

  • Matter plan and task list
  • Limitation and court deadlines diarised
  • Document templates pre-loaded

Discovery, Research & Document Collection

The firm gathers evidence, financial disclosure, contracts, identification, or whatever the file requires; conducts legal research; and prepares a working file plan.

  • Secure client portal for document upload
  • Statutory and case-law research (CanLII, Westlaw, Lexis)
  • Witness interviews where applicable

Strategy & Drafting

The lawyer drafts pleadings, contracts, wills, agreements, demand letters, or immigration applications. All drafts undergo internal review before being sent to the client.

  • Precedent library and clause bank
  • Two-stage internal review
  • Plain-language client summaries

Negotiation & Representation

The firm represents the client before opposing counsel, government agencies (IRCC, Land Titles), tribunals, or courts — pursuing settlement first where appropriate.

  • Mediation and collaborative practice
  • Court appearances and hearings
  • Regular client status updates

Execution & Closing

Documents are signed, registered, filed, or submitted. Funds are disbursed from trust. Title transfers, divorce judgments, probate grants, or PR confirmations are obtained.

  • E-signing and remote commissioning
  • Trust disbursement with full reconciliation
  • Registration with Land Titles, courts, or IRCC

Billing & Collections

Time entries are captured daily; invoices are issued per the retainer schedule; trust funds are applied per LSA rules; outstanding receivables are followed up systematically.

  • Detailed dockets and transparent narratives
  • Electronic invoicing and online payment
  • Monthly trust reconciliation

File Closing & Reporting Letter

Upon completion the firm issues a reporting letter summarising the work done, outcomes, and any ongoing client obligations such as updating a will after major life events.

  • Final reporting letter
  • Return of original documents
  • Closed-file archiving (10+ years per LSA rules)

Post-Engagement & Client Retention

The firm requests feedback, invites reviews, schedules periodic check-ins (estate refresh, immigration renewal), and continues to add value via newsletters and seminars.

  • NPS survey and testimonials
  • Annual "legal check-up" reminders
  • Referrals and alumni program
