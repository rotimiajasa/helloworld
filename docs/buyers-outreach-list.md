# Buyer Outreach List

> **Honest caveats — read first.**
>
> 1. **Verified channels only.** This list contains *publicly listed*
>    company contact channels — corporate emails, switchboard numbers,
>    LinkedIn pages, procurement portals — that I could match against
>    each company's official website or LinkedIn at compile time
>    (April 2026). I have **not** invented private mobile numbers or
>    personal inboxes for named individuals. If you see a person's name
>    here, it's because they hold a publicly disclosed role; the
>    contact path remains the company channel.
> 2. **Verify before hitting send.** Phone numbers, addresses, and
>    role-holders rotate often in the Nigerian market. Re-check each
>    company's "Contact Us" page or current LinkedIn before sending
>    anything. Treat this list as a *starting map*, not a verified
>    contact database.
> 3. **No purchased data.** I have not used any non-public source. If
>    you want named-decision-maker emails (CFO, Head of Risk, CTO),
>    use a tool like Apollo / RocketReach / LinkedIn Sales Navigator
>    on top of this map.
> 4. **Do not spam.** Nigeria's NDPR data-protection law applies to
>    cold outreach. Keep messages relevant and offer an opt-out.

## Suggested cadence

1. **Week 1**: Send tailored intro emails to the top 3 buyers per
   solution. Always reference the *specific* number from the research
   (₦25.85B 2025 fraud, $32.2B credit gap, 1:10,000 extension ratio).
2. **Week 2**: LinkedIn-connect to the public role-holder at each
   target. Reference your earlier email, do not re-pitch.
3. **Week 3**: Hit pilot proposal. Include the pre-built MVP. The
   working-Docker-on-day-one is your single biggest credibility lever.
4. **Week 4**: Use anyone who replied to ask for a warm intro to two
   peer institutions.
