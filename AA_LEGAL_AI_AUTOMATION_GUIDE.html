Section 01 — The Case for AI

Why AI Now, And Why It Matters For AA Legal

Roughly 40–60% of a typical lawyer’s day is spent on tasks that don’t require legal judgement — drafting boilerplate, summarising documents, hunting through emails, chasing payments, and re-typing intake data. AI can quietly eliminate most of that friction while keeping a human firmly in charge of every decision that matters.

⏱

Reclaim Billable Hours

Time → Value

Automate intake, drafting first cuts, and document review so lawyers can spend more time on strategy, advocacy, and client relationships.

$

Lower Cost-To-Serve

Margin → Up

Flat-fee files (wills, real-estate, simple immigration) become dramatically more profitable when AI handles 50–70% of the production work.

★

Better Client Experience

Speed + Clarity

24/7 intake, instant document explanations in plain English, faster turnaround, and proactive status updates without staff overhead.

!

Reduce Risk & Errors

Quality → Up

AI-assisted deadline tracking, conflict checks, and second-eye review reduce the chance of missed limitations, fees, or clauses.

Section 02 — Automation Catalogue

14 High-Value Use Cases for AA Legal

Each card below describes a discrete automation: what it does, the recommended technology, the steps to implement, and the business impact. Use these as building blocks — most firms start with 2–3 and grow from there.

💬

1 · 24/7 AI Intake Assistant

Reception · After-hours

What it does: A chatbot on aalegal.ca greets visitors, asks structured questions, captures matter type, urgency, and contact info, and books a consult slot — even at 2 AM.

How to Automate
  • Use an LLM-based chatbot (e.g., Smith.ai, LawDroid, or a custom GPT)
  • Train it with practice-area FAQs and intake forms
  • Integrate with Clio Grow / Calendly for booking
  • Push leads into the CRM with conflict-check pre-screen
Guardrails
  • Always discloses it is an AI assistant
  • Never gives legal advice — only collects information
  • Escalates urgent matters to a human
Impact: HighEffort: Low
📝

2 · AI-Assisted Drafting

Wills · Contracts · Letters

What it does: Generates first drafts of routine documents — wills, separation agreements, residential purchase contracts, demand letters — from a structured intake form.

How to Automate
  • Build precedent templates in Word / Google Docs
  • Use a tool like Gavel, LawYaw, or HotDocs to map intake fields to clauses
  • Add an AI layer (Harvey, Spellbook, or Microsoft Copilot for Word) for clause refinement and plain-English summaries
  • Lawyer reviews & signs off before client delivery
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
🔍

3 · Contract & Document Review

Real Estate · Commercial

What it does: Scans inbound contracts (purchase agreements, leases, condo docs, separation drafts) and flags missing clauses, unusual terms, deadlines, and risks.

How to Automate
  • Use Spellbook, Kira, or Harvey for clause extraction
  • Configure a checklist per matter type (e.g., condo doc review checklist)
  • AI produces a red-flag report; lawyer reviews
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
⚖

4 · AI Legal Research

Case Law · Statutes

What it does: Answers legal questions with citations to Canadian case law and statutes, drafts memos, and surfaces analogous decisions.

How to Automate
  • Subscribe to Westlaw Edge Canada AI, Lexis+ AI, or CanLII
  • Use Harvey or Vincent AI for memo drafting
  • Always verify citations — never rely on raw LLM output
Impact: HighEffort: Low
✉

5 · Email Triage & Drafting

Inbox Hygiene

What it does: Sorts incoming email by urgency & matter, summarises long threads, and drafts reply suggestions in the lawyer’s voice.

How to Automate
  • Microsoft Copilot for Outlook or Google Gemini in Gmail
  • Custom rules to auto-tag by client & matter
  • Daily AI-summarised inbox briefing at 7 AM
Impact: MediumEffort: Low
⏲

6 · Passive Time Capture & Billing Narratives

Revenue Leakage

What it does: Detects time spent in documents, emails, and calls; generates compliant billing narratives; flags non-billable items.

How to Automate
  • Clio Duo, Smokeball AutoTime, or TimeSolv AI
  • Train the model on AA Legal’s preferred narrative style
  • Lawyer approves the daily timesheet in one click
Impact: HighEffort: Low
🛡

7 · Conflict Check & KYC Automation

Compliance

What it does: Runs fuzzy-match conflict checks across past matters, automates LSA-compliant ID verification, and screens for PEPs / sanctions lists.

How to Automate
  • Persona, Onfido, or Trulioo for ID verification
  • Clio Manage or LawConnect for conflict search with AI fuzzy match
  • Automated FINTRAC screening API
Impact: HighEffort: Medium
📅

8 · Deadline & Court-Rules Engine

Risk Control

What it does: Calculates limitation periods, court filing deadlines, and IRCC processing timelines automatically from the matter type and key dates.

How to Automate
  • LawToolBox, CalendarRules, or Clio’s court-rules add-on
  • AI parses orders / pleadings and auto-creates calendar entries
  • SMS & email reminders 30 / 14 / 3 / 1 day(s) ahead
Impact: HighEffort: Medium

CX · Retention

What it does: Sends automatic milestone updates ("Your offer was accepted", "Your PR application reached IRCC stage 2"), in plain English.

High Volume

How an AI Drafts a Will — Step by Step

Many people fear AI as a "black box". Here is exactly what happens when AA Legal uses AI to draft a will, in plain language.

From client questionnaire → finished will

2Data MappingAnswers populate variables (name, executor, beneficiaries...)

What the AI Never Decides

The AI does not choose beneficiaries, draft trust structures for tax planning, advise on capacity, or sign anything. It is a production assistant — the lawyer remains the brain of the operation.

Section 08 — Ethics & Guardrails

Non-Negotiables When Using AI in a Canadian Law Firm

The Law Society of Alberta has issued guidance on generative AI. AA Legal’s use of AI must respect every one of the following.

Competence

Lawyers must understand the AI tools they use, their limitations, and verify all outputs — especially citations.

Confidentiality

Only use AI tools that contractually prohibit training on client data and offer Canadian or SOC-2-compliant data hosting.

Privilege

Never paste privileged content into consumer LLMs. Use enterprise tiers with DPAs in place.

Supervision

Every AI output is reviewed by a qualified lawyer. AI does not sign, file, or advise.

Candour

Disclose AI use to clients where material, and in court where required.

Fees

Bill fairly: efficiency gains from AI should reduce client fees where charged by the hour.

Bias & Fairness

Be alert to AI-introduced bias, particularly in family, criminal, and immigration matters.

Records & Audit

Keep an audit log of significant AI uses on each file. Update retainer language to disclose AI tools.

AA Legal’s AI Use Policy — One Sentence

"AI helps us work faster and clearer, but every legal judgement, every document delivered to a client, and every word filed with a court is owned, reviewed, and signed by a human lawyer at AA Legal."
