## 1. Executive Summary

> **Carton Bank** is a corrugated-carton (OCC) recovery and MSME-finance venture launched by **Plastic Solution** to convert recurring carton waste into **cash, savings, and working-capital loans** for Nigerian micro and small businesses.

### 🎯 The Opportunity
Nigeria generates an estimated **600,000 – 800,000 metric tonnes** of corrugated containers annually, with formal recovery below **35%**. The supply chain is dominated by informal, unbanked actors — creating a clear opening for a digitized, finance-enabled aggregator.

### 💡 The Proposition
Carton Bank lets MSMEs:

| # | Capability | Mechanism |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Exchange carton waste for cash | Instant wallet credit at agreed grade-based price |
| 2 | Save earnings automatically | Co-branded "Carton SafeLock" with Piggyvest |
| 3 | Access working-capital loans | Disbursed by LAPO; repaid in carton deliveries |
| 4 | Track everything digitally | Pakam-powered app, USSD & WhatsApp bot |
| 5 | Earn ESG/EPR/Carbon recognition | Verified recovery sold to producer schemes |

### 📊 Headline Numbers (Year 1 → Year 3)

| Metric | Year 1 | Year 3 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Tonnes recovered | 8,000 | 36,000 |

### Stage 1 — Onboarding (KYC + Waste Profiling)

**Channels:** Field agents · Pakam app · USSD short code · WhatsApp bot · Hub walk-ins

| Step | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Capture business name, BVN/NIN, geotagged address, premises photo | Field Agent |
| 1.2 | Estimate weekly carton output (2-week observation) | Field Agent + Generator |
| 1.3 | Tier the MSME — **T1:** <50 kg/wk · **T2:** 50–500 kg/wk · **T3:** >500 kg/wk | System |
| 1.4 | Issue **Carton Bank ID** + digital wallet linked to LAPO/Piggyvest | Pakam |

---

### Stage 2 — Collection

**Three service tiers — generator chooses based on convenience vs. price:**

| Model | Price to Generator | Logistics Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🚶 **Drop-off** at hub | Highest (Grade A premium) | None | Markets, large clusters |
| 📅 **Scheduled pickup** | Mid | Routed | Supermarkets, restaurants |
| 🚛 **On-demand pickup** (app) | Lowest net | High | E-commerce, occasional users |

**Standard Operating Procedure:**

1. Generator **flattens** cartons (price premium).
2. Agent **weighs** on calibrated digital scale.
3. **Contamination check** — reject wax-coated, food-soiled, laminated.
4. **Grade assigned** — A (clean OCC), B (mixed), C (rejected).
5. **QR-coded receipt** issued; wallet credited **in real time**.

---

### Stage 3 — Aggregation & Processing

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| 3.1 | Transport to nearest hub (start Lagos: Ikeja, Apapa, Lekki) |
| 3.2 | Sort by grade |
| 3.3 | Bale using horizontal/vertical balers (400–500 kg bales) |
| 3.4 | Store under cover — moisture **<12%** (mills reject wet bales) |
| 3.5 | Log to Pakam inventory: batch ID, weight, grade, source |

---

### Stage 4 — Offtake & Sales

1. Weekly tonnage forecast issued to mills.
2. Contracts: **spot + 3-month fixed-price framework** with BEL Papers / Bel Impex.
3. Logistics to mill (own fleet or 3PL: Kobo360, Truckr).
4. Quality reconciliation at mill weighbridge.
5. Payment within **7–14 days** — invoice-discounting fallback via Sterling/Wema.

---

### Stage 5 — Carton-Collateralized Lending ⭐ *The Differentiator*

#### Loan Product Specification

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Ticket size | ₦20,000 – ₦500,000 |
| Tenor | 30 / 60 / 90 days |
| Interest | 4–6% flat per month (LAPO regulated) |
| Repayment | Weekly carton deliveries auto-deducted from wallet + optional cash top-up |
| Eligibility | ≥8 weeks of verified carton-supply history |

#### Lending Flow

```
   MSME applies (Pakam/USSD)
            │
            ▼
   Carton Bank credit score:
   ─ Supply consistency
   ─ Volume
   ─ Tenure on platform              ────▶  Approve / Decline
   ─ Business type
            │
            ▼
   LAPO disburses to wallet
   (Carton Bank earns 2–3% origination)
            │
            ▼
   Each carton delivery offsets
   loan balance at locked price
            │
            ▼
   Default fallback:
   BVN-linked direct debit
```

> **Risk-share structure:** Carton Bank guarantees a **10% first-loss tranche**; LAPO funds the rest. This is bankable and keeps incentives aligned.

## 5. Key Partnerships

### 5.1 Pakam — Green-Tech Platform
- **Role:** Digital backbone — user/agent apps, wallet, geolocation, scheduling, ledger, ESG dashboard.
- **Commercial:** Revenue share (5–8% of transaction value) **or** SaaS + per-pickup fee.
- **Integration:** APIs to Carton Bank ERP, IoT weighbridges, MFB core banking.

### 5.2 LAPO Microfinance Bank
- **Role:** Licensed lender, KYC, disbursement, collections.
- **Commercial:** Carton Bank originates & services; LAPO funds. Net interest split **~60/40** (LAPO/Carton Bank). Carton Bank holds **10% first-loss**.

### 5.3 Piggyvest
- **Role:** Wallet + auto-savings ("Carton SafeLock").
- **Commercial:** Co-branded product, referral fee, float share.

### 5.4 Paper Mills (BEL · Bel Impex · Dangote Paper)
- **Role:** Primary offtakers.
- **Commercial:** Fixed-tonnage contracts with quarterly price review + volume rebates.

### 5.5 EPR PROs (FBRA · Alliance for Responsible Recycling)
- **Role:** Buy verified recovery tonnage for producer-obligation discharge.
- **Commercial:** ₦/tonne credit fee.

### 5.6 Logistics (Kobo360 · Truckr · In-house Fleet)
- **Role:** Inter-hub + hub-to-mill movement.
- **Commercial:** Per-trip or per-tonne-km rates.

## 8. Key Considerations & Risk Register

### 8.1 Commercial Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| OCC price volatility (global pulp index) | High | 3-month forward contracts; price-collar formulas |
| Mill payment delays | Medium | Factoring facility; diversified buyers; export option |
| MSME loan defaults | Medium | Carton-flow collateral; LTV ≤ 60% of 90-day supply; MFB risk-share |
| Informal aggregator competition | Medium | Price-premium on Grade A; non-cash perks (loans, savings, insurance) |

### 8.2 Operational Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Rainy-season moisture damage | High | Covered yards; moisture meters; dehumidified storage |
| Fire hazard (paper bales) | High | Insurance, suppression systems, NEMA-compliant siting |
| Weight fraud at collection | Medium | Calibrated tamper-seal scales; audit team; spot checks |
| Bale theft in transit | Medium | GPS-tracked trucks; QR-sealed bales |

### 8.3 Regulatory & Compliance

- ✅ Register with **NESREA** as a recycler
- ✅ **LAWMA PSP** carton-recycler permit (Lagos)
- ✅ **LASEPA** environmental impact compliance
- ✅ Join an **EPR PRO** (e.g., FBRA) — mandatory under the NESREA EPR Regulation
- ✅ **NDPR** data-privacy compliance (handled via Pakam)
- ⚠️ **No direct lending license needed** — LAPO is the regulated lender. *(Critical structural point.)*

### 8.4 Strategic Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Cannibalization of Plastic Solution core focus | Separate P&L; dedicated GM; shared back-office only |
| Brand confusion | Sub-brand: *"Carton Bank — a Plastic Solution initiative"* |
| Donor/grant dependency | Operational profitability by Month 18; grants accelerate, not sustain |

## 13. Long-Term Vision

> Carton Bank evolves into **"Material Bank"** — a multi-stream circular-economy wallet covering **OCC, PET, HDPE, aluminum, and e-waste** — positioning **Plastic Solution as the dominant waste-as-collateral financial infrastructure in West Africa.**

**Potential exits:**
- Strategic sale to an FMCG-backed Producer Responsibility Organization
- Merger with a regional recycler
- Listing on the NGX Growth Board

### Why This Wins

Carton Bank's edge is **not** that it collects cartons — many do.
Its edge is that it **turns recurring waste into a financial asset class** for MSMEs, underwritten by:

- 📡 **Data** that Pakam captures
- 🏦 **Balance sheet** that LAPO / Piggyvest provide
- 🚛 **Logistics** that Plastic Solution already runs
- 📑 **Compliance value** that NESREA EPR mandates

> **Execution discipline** on pricing, moisture, loan underwriting, and mill relationships will determine whether this becomes a **₦5B/year business by Year 5** — or a stalled pilot.
>
> **Start lean** (one hub · one mill · one MFB). **Prove unit economics.** **Then scale aggressively.**
